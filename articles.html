Are you looking for better ways to focus on growing your business’s bottom-line? If so, one of the easiest decisions any business owner can make is outsourcing their accounting operations. There are many reasons to do this besides just being rid of the hassle that comes along with bookkeeping. Let’s take a look at a few.

• Cut down on costs. Outsourced accounting operations save you money by eliminating costly benefit packages to a full-time or part-time employee. When you outsource accounting, you only pay for the actual accounting, nothing else. This will save payroll costs ex Gosi, LMRA & Visa fees, infrastructure cost ex PC & software license.

Cost analyst state that outsourced accounting vs. in-house bookkeeping can save up to 40% in monthly costs.

• Reduced Fraud. According to Association of Certified Fraud Examiner’s study released in 2012, most common victims of fraud are privately owned small & medium businesses with fraud amounts of thousands This is due the fact that most small & medium companies don’t have access to a controller who could look at the KPI and metrics which show abnormal activity in the transactional and billing data. With Outsourced accounting small businesses can afford a controller who can look for fraud signs and implement proper fraud protection controls.

• Accountants know accounting. By outsourcing your accounting operations to a professional accounting firm, you’re effectively hiring a team of experts. By outsourcing have the most experienced and qualified individuals on your side, right where you need them.

• A team not an accountant. No matter how you look at it, having a team of professional accountants working on your books is going to be better than having an in-house employee keeping them. Whether it’s you, a part-time accountant or full-time employee. By outsource you actually will have team as the work of Accounting firm reviewed internally by more one person.

• Scalable options. Last, but certainly not least, we have scalable options. When you outsource your accounting operations, it becomes simple to expand and grow your business or find places to cut down on spending. Accounting firms can provide you with lists of options, giving you valuable feedback and suggestions that will raise your profits at the end of the day.
